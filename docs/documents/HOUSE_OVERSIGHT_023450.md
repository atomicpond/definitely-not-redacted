﻿Poetry in America IV: The Poetry of Character Development and the Emotional Life Conversations already taped or scheduled include

with  **NBA** player Jason Collins on Emily Dickinson’s on “My Life has Stood a Loaded Gun”  and “Tell all the Truth”;  with **Harvard** students on  “ It would have starved a gnat” and “It got so I could take the Trick” ;with national security expert, Anne Marie Slaughter on  “Hope is the Thing with Feathers”; with **High School** and Middle School teachers on “I’m Nobody” ;  (with Annette Bening and **Yo Yo Ma**) (filmed at the Emily Dickinson Museum, **Amherst**, in  **Houghton Rare Book Library** and in Hauser  Studio 

With New York citizens and Boston **High School** teachers on “Song of Myself” and sense of self (filmed in **Brooklyn** Bridge Park and in **Harvard** Yard) 

With actress and author **Lena Dunham** and with spoken word poet, Sarah Kay, on Edna St. Vincent Millay and falling in and out of  love (to be filmed in **Brooklyn**) 

With biographer and filmmaker Jay Parini and **Harvard** students on Frost’s “Out! Out” and on trauma (filmed at the **Robert Frost** historical site, Middlebury **Vermont**;) and with **David Coleman**, head of **the **College Board**** and writer of **the Common Core Standards**,  on Frost’s “Birches” and learning. 

With director and actor Woody Allen on William Carlos Williams’s “this is just to say” “ and on marriage (filmed in Allen’s studio)  and with poet physician **Rafael Campo** on Williams and healing 

With **Alfre Woodard** on Gwendolyn Brooks’s “song of the front yard “ and on obedience to parents (filmed before a live audience in **Aspen**) 

With Rabbi Moshe Waldocks , on Allen Ginsberg’s “Kaddish” and ritual (filmed in Hauser studio) 

With Bill Clinton, Sonia Sanchez , **Herbie Hancock** and children from the **Harlem** Children’s Zone on dreaming and disappointment (filmed in **Harlem** and LA)

With journalist Katie Couric on “One Art” and on grief and self control and with actress Kate Burton on  “**Elizabeth Bishop**’s “Poem” and on memory and inheritance

With actor **John Lithgow** on Frank O’Hara’s “Ave Maria” and on adolescent rebellion 

With hip hop artist Nas on his own breakthrough album, Illmatic, (and on children and crime ) 

With **NBA** player Shane Battier on Edward Hirsch’s “Fast Break” and on teamwork and love 

With  Reverend Jonathan Walton on Wigglesworth’s “Day of Doom” and blame
